# Glossary of Period Medical Terms (15th–16th Century)

This glossary provides historically accurate definitions of medical terms used in primary accounts of the English Sweating Sickness. Definitions reflect period semantics, not modern clinical categories.

---

## **Sweate / Sweat**
Not equivalent to modern perspiration. Often implies a pathological flux or systemic purgation. May include odor, discoloration, or altered consistency.

## **Stinking Sweat**
A foul or corrupted sweat. In period medical language, indicates internal putrefaction, toxic corruption, or systemic imbalance. A key diagnostic marker.

## **Oppression**
A sense of pressure, constriction, or heaviness, especially in the chest. Often associated with impaired respiration or autonomic distress.

## **Inward Heat**
Internal burning or heat without external fever. Represents dysregulated internal processes, not infection-driven pyrexia.

## **Flux**
Uncontrolled discharge or purgation. Could refer to sweat, stool, urine, or generalized bodily evacuation.

## **Prostration**
Sudden collapse or loss of strength. A hallmark of the Sweating Sickness onset.

## **Ague**
A shaking or trembling fit. Not necessarily fever; often autonomic instability.

## **Faintness / Swoon**
Loss of consciousness or near-collapse. Often described in early phases.

## **Corruption**
A period term for internal spoilage, putrefaction, or toxic alteration of bodily humors.

## **Malignity**
A harmful or poisonous quality. Not equivalent to malignancy in modern oncology.

---

This glossary is designed to prevent anachronistic flattening and support accurate interpretation of primary sources.
